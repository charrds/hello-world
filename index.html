#100DaysofCode Commitment Framework

The #100DaysofCode challenge is woven as a non-negotiable anchor throughout this entire 18-month plan. It builds public accountability, grows your Twitter/X following, and creates an undeniable archive of your commitment.

⚡ The Non-Negotiable Rules
  • Code for at least 1 hour every day — weekdays AND weekends
  • Post daily on X/Twitter using #100DaysofCode and #buildinpublic
  • Log your work in a public GitHub repo (your coding journal)
  • Encourage at least 2 other people in the challenge per week
  • No breaks longer than 1 day — restart the count if you miss 2 consecutive days

Content Strategy
Blog Editorial Calendar — All 18 Months

Publish one post every two weeks — 36 posts across 18 months. Alternate between Technical Deep Dives (for developers — builds credibility) and Reflective / Business Posts (for executives and clients — builds authority). Both audiences matter for your CTO trajectory.

📐 The 3-Format Blog Strategy
  • Tutorial / How-To (40%) — "How I built X with Y" — gets organic Google traffic, demonstrates skill
  • Retrospective / Reflection (30%) — "Month N done — what I learned" — builds community, earns social shares
  • Opinion / Analysis (30%) — "Why every PM should understand RAG" — builds thought leadership, attracts press
